After trying a couple dozen Chrome extensions that claim to group tabs, I realized none of them worked quite the way I wanted. I wanted something simple, a tool that groups tabs by domain, automatically, with zero setup or configuration. That's it.

Instead of continuing to dig through endless listings, I decided to build it myself. Meet Tab Constellation, an extension that automatically organizes your tabs by domain, keeping your browser clean and your workflow focused, no extra steps required.

## See It in Action

Curious how Tab Constellation works? Check out this short demo video:

[Watch the Loom Demo](https://www.loom.com/share/73272fd435a646c386d1d614bb1da703)

## Why I Built It

While working on this project, I was heavily inspired by the idea of opinionated tools like Prettier. It takes a strong stance on how your code should be formatted, removes a lot of unnecessary decision-making, and lets you focus on what matters: creating quality software.

That's exactly what I wanted Tab Constellation to be. If you're like me and you tend to keep tons of browser tabs open, you probably don't want another dashboard or a dozen knobs to turn, you just want your tabs automatically grouped in a simple, predictable way so you can stay focused.
